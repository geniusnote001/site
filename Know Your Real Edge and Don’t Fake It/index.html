Know Your Real Edge and Don’t Fake It

In a in a cozy suburban, there lived a man named Thomas. He had always been a hardworking individual, dedicated to his career and providing for his family. However, deep down, he knew that he was not living up to his true potential. Thomas had a nagging feeling that he was holding himself back, compromising his fullest gift, all because of his fears.

One evening, as Thomas sat alone in his office, surrounded by stacks of paperwork, he couldn't ignore the voice inside him any longer. He realized that he had been catering to his fears, limiting his income and stifling his creativity. The thought of taking risks, of venturing into new territory, scared him immensely.

In an attempt to confront his fears, Thomas decided to have a conversation with himself. He spoke out loud, acknowledging the truth about his current situation. "I know I could be earning more money," he admitted, "but I am too lazy to put in the extra hours it would take. I'm afraid of failure, afraid that I'll be left with nothing. I've spent so many years building my career, and starting fresh terrifies me. Yet, deep down, I know I'm not truly happy with what I'm doing. I have so many unexplored interests, so much untapped potential."

As Thomas voiced his fears, he felt a weight lift off his shoulders. He realized that he had been hiding from his fears for far too long, pretending they didn't exist. In doing so, he had lost the trust of his friends and colleagues. They could sense his fear, even if he couldn't admit it himself. Thomas understood that living at his edge meant being honest, both with himself and with others.

Determined to make a change, Thomas embarked on a journey of self-discovery. He sought out new opportunities, pushing himself beyond his comfort zone. It wasn't easy. Fear still lingered, threatening to hold him back. But Thomas knew that a fearful man who acknowledged his fears was far more trustworthy than one who denied them.

Slowly but surely, Thomas began to make progress. He found the courage to take risks, to pursue his passions, and to explore different avenues of earning a living. He let go of the safety net that had kept him comfortable but stagnant. Although uncertainty loomed over him, Thomas realized that the only way to truly live at his edge was to lean into his fear, embracing it instead of running from it.

As time went on, Thomas transformed into an inspiration for those around him. His friends and colleagues saw the change in him—the aliveness, depth, and energy that radiated from his being. They witnessed his commitment to living authentically, and they trusted him to guide them on their own journeys.

Thomas's story serves as a reminder to us all. We, too, have our own edges, places where we compromise our fullest gifts due to fear. It is essential to recognize and acknowledge those fears, for only then can we begin to overcome them. By living with our lips pressed against our fears, neither pulling back nor violating them aggressively, we can embark on a path of authenticity and inspire others to do the same.

So, dear reader, what is your edge? What fears are holding you back from reaching your true potential? Take a moment to reflect and make a pledge to yourself—to honor your edge, to be honest with yourself and others, and to lean into your fears, knowing that true growth and fulfillment await on the other side.
